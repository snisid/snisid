# SNISID: Forensic Stream Replay Engine

The Forensic Replay Engine (FRE) provides a "National Time Machine," allowing investigators to reconstruct events, retrain AI models on historical data, and audit past system states with absolute precision.

---

## 1. Replay Architecture: The Isolated Sandbox

To prevent replay events from interfering with live production systems, SNISID uses **Isolated Replay Environments**.

### 1.1. Components
- **The Replay Controller**: Orchestrates the selection of topics and time-ranges.
- **Tiered Storage Fetcher**: Pulls historical segments from **Sovereign Object Storage** (MinIO).
- **Virtual Replay Topics**: Historical data is re-published to transient, high-speed topics (e.g., `replay.national.auth.v1`).
- **Sandbox Workloads**: Temporary instances of microservices (e.g., Fraud Engine) are spun up specifically to process the replay stream.

---

## 2. Event Reconstruction Workflows

### 2.1. Time-Travel Analysis
1.  **Selection**: Investigator selects a `Start_Time` and `End_Time` for a specific `Identity_ID` or `Domain`.
2.  **Hydration**: The FRE identifies the relevant Kafka segments in Tiered Storage.
3.  **Sequencing**: Events are streamed in their original temporal order, preserved by the `Sovereign_Timestamp`.
4.  **Reconstruction**: The sandbox service rebuilds its state (e.g., a Neo4j graph sub-tree) as it appeared at that specific moment in history.

### 2.2. Forensic Auditing
- **Muted Side-Effects**: During a replay, the sandbox is configured with **"Mute-by-Design"**; it cannot send real-world notifications, trigger payments, or affect live user trust scores.
- **Differential Analysis**: The engine compares the "Historical Decision" (recorded in audit logs) with the "Replay Decision" (generated by new/updated AI models) to identify missed threats.

---

## 3. Replay Security & Controls

Replaying national identity data is a sensitive operation.

- **Access Control**: Replay requests require **"Four-Eyes" Authorization** and a valid judicial warrant code.
- **Data Scoping**: Investigators are restricted to specific topic prefixes and time-ranges.
- **Audit Tracking**: Every replay action is itself recorded in the **Sovereign Audit Ledger**, including the specific filters used and the identity of the auditor.
- **Crypto-Isolation**: Replay data remains encrypted using historical BEKs (Backup Encryption Keys) until it enters the secure sandbox memory.

---

## 4. Operational Tooling Design

- **The Timeline Viewer**: A UI component for the National SOC that allows "scrubbing" through event density over time.
- **Replay Scheduler**: Management of concurrent replay jobs to ensure they do not saturate the Tiered Storage egress bandwidth.
- **Consistency Validator**: Tool to verify that a replayed state matches the historical checkpoint (Snapshot) recorded at that time.
