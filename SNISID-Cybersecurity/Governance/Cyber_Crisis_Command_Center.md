# SNISID Cyber Crisis Command Center (C4)

## 1. Objective
To provide a strategic command and control structure for managing national-level cyber crises that threaten the continuity of government and critical infrastructure.

## 2. Operational Scope
The C4 is activated when an incident exceeds the operational capacity of the SOC/CERT or when there is a significant impact on national stability.

## 3. Core Capabilities

| Function | Description |
| :--- | :--- |
| **National Coordination** | Syncing efforts between military, intelligence, and civilian agencies. |
| **Real-time Dashboards** | High-level visualization of "National Cyber Health" for decision makers. |
| **Incident Escalation** | Formal path to elevate technical findings to strategic decisions. |
| **Government Communication** | Secure channels for updating the Head of State and Cabinet. |
| **Emergency Authority** | Ability to order temporary shutdowns of specific networks to save others. |

## 4. Command Structure
- **Strategic Level:** National Cyber Council (Policy, Law, Political).
- **Operational Level:** C4 Director (Coordination, Resource Allocation).
- **Tactical Level:** SOC/CERT Leads (Technical execution, Forensics).

## 5. Crisis Levels
- **Level 1 (Green):** Routine operations.
- **Level 2 (Yellow):** Significant incident; CERT lead activated.
- **Level 3 (Orange):** Major breach; C4 activated, agency heads notified.
- **Level 4 (Red):** National emergency; Full C4 activation, government continuity protocols enabled.

## 6. Continuity of Government (COG)
The C4 ensures that even under a total cyber-blackout, the country can:
- Communicate via emergency satellite/analog channels.
- Maintain critical energy and water services.
- Execute essential sovereign functions.
