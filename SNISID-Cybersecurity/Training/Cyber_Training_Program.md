# SNISID National Cyber Training Program

## 1. Objective
To build and maintain a highly skilled national workforce capable of operating the SOC, CERT, and other cyberdefense functions.

## 2. Training Tracks

| Track | Target Role | Key Focus Areas |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **SOC Analyst** | Tier 1/2 Analysts | Log analysis, SIEM usage, Triage, Phishing analysis. |
| **DFIR Specialist** | Tier 3 Analysts | Memory forensics, disk imaging, timeline reconstruction. |
| **Threat Hunter** | Security Engineers | Hypothesis generation, KQL/Lucene queries, MITRE ATT&CK. |
| **Kubernetes Security** | Cloud Engineers | Pod security policies, NetworkPolicies, Falco rules. |
| **IAM Security** | Identity Architects | OAuth2, OIDC, Zero Trust policies, MFA implementation. |

## 3. Training Methodology
- **Theoretical Foundation:** Online courses and certifications (e.g., SANS, OSCP).
- **Hands-on Labs:** Use of "Cyber Ranges" to practice on mirrored environments.
- **CTF (Capture The Flag):** Regular internal competitions to encourage skill growth.
- **Shadowing:** Junior analysts working alongside senior DFIR specialists.

## 4. Certification Requirements
To maintain "Active Duty" status in the SOC, analysts must obtain:
- **Entry Level:** Security+ or equivalent.
- **Mid Level:** GCIH (Incident Handler) or OSCP.
- **Advanced Level:** GCFA (Forensics Analyst) or GXPN (Exploitation).

## 5. Continuous Education
- **Weekly Briefs:** Reviewing the latest threat intelligence reports.
- **Post-Mortem Reviews:** Learning from real incidents handled by the CERT.
- **External Workshops:** Participation in global cybersecurity summits.
